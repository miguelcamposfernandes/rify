Best practices to identify fake news

1. Read

Never share anything without reading!

Being critical is a skill you should adapt, not only for identifying misinformation but also for your day-to-day life.

2. Be Critical

It's too good to be true? Than it's probably fake.

We should always read beyond the title, this prevents us from sharing misinformation and old news, which ultimately also creates disinformation.

  • Can it be true information but decontextualized?
  • It is alarmist, sensationalist, improbable, without contradictory?
  • Who is the information for and what kind of reaction do they expect?
  • Who benefits from this content and who gets harmed?
3. Evaluate the source

Search the origin!

Always try to track it down to the origin. And quickly evaluate their credibility and professionalism, towards the corresponding topic.

4. Search other sources

Perform a Google search

Quickly copy the news title and make a Google, Bing, Yandex or Baidu search to then compare it to other trustful sources, and make a conclusion.

5. Search for verification

Try Snopes or Bellingcat

Check if the information has already been denied/clarified or confirmed by some verification pages like Snopes or bellingcat.
