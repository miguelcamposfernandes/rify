How it works - How are we approaching fake news detection?

The spread of misinformation has been a huge topic all over the globe, and many indivuals and organizations are trying to come up with an effective solution towards this problem.

The far most common approach is to use machine learning, a branch of artificial intelligence, to detect false information. In short the way it works is by taking lots of fake and real news, to then try detect patterns. We also tried coming up with a acceptable solution using A.I, but we ended up thinking of a new approach, which tries to replicate the human behaviour to some extent.

Replicating the human behaviour

When a news article seems to be dubious, we tend to make further research about that event. We usually start by performing a Google Search and we then see, if other trustful news publishers also are posting about event, and if that is the case it's probably true.
And this is what our algorithm is trying to replicate:

1. Perform a Google Search

We start by taking the article's title inputted by the user to then perform a basic Google search.

2. Check if the sources are trustful

We then take the first 10 results and compare each of them to a list of trustful news publishers we have for each country.

3. Compare the title similarity

Of course it would not be enough to just check if the results are republished by trustful sources, as Google always shows something no matter what you search. For example we tried searching Trump ate a child in Google and the first 2 results were from considerable trustful news publishers:

So, to undergo this little issue, we just compare the setence similarity of the inputted news title and the trustful news title. We do this by calculating the cosine similarity, which is easily done by Python Natural-Language-Processing library, Spacy.

4. Output the result

Finally we output the result based on these parameters:

  • 0 trustful sources unreliable with high certainty
  • 1 trustful sources unreliable with low certainty
  • 2 trustful sources reliable with low to medium certainty
  • 3 trustful sources reliable with medium certainty
  • 4 trustful sources reliable with high certainty
  • 5+ trustful sources reliable with very high certainty
